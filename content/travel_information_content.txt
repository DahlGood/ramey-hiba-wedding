Arriving in Morocco
When you arrive at the airport, we recommend taking out cash from an ATM before heading to your hotel or riad. Morocco uses Moroccan dirhams, and it is necessary to have cash on hand for taxis, tips, small shops, and local purchases. When using the ATM, decline any currency conversion offered on the screen. Choose to be charged in Moroccan dirhams instead, which usually helps avoid extra conversion fees from the ATM provider.

Driving & Car Rentals
Driving in Morocco
If you plan to rent a car, please keep these quick tips in mind before hitting the road.



Always buckle up

Seatbelts are required and police may stop drivers or passengers who are not wearing one.



Yield at roundabouts

Cars already in the roundabout have the right of way. Wait until you have clear space before entering.



Give yourself extra time

Rabat is a busy city, and traffic can be heavy. Plan to leave a bit earlier than usual so you have plenty of time to arrive comfortably.
